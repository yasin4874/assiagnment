1. Q: What should every PC technician know how to do?
A: Take a computer apart and put it back together again.
2. Q: Why is planning important before working inside a computer?
A: It helps stay organized, avoid losing parts, and ensure safe disassembly and reassembly.
3. Q: Name three safety precautions when preparing to disassemble a computer.
A: Remove loose jewelry, don't touch board chips, and avoid stacking boards.
4. Q: What tools or materials should be avoided when touching microchips?
A: Magnetized screwdrivers.
5. Q: What should be done to prevent electrical shock or damage?
A: Don't work inside a powered-on computer and consider components like monitors and power supplies as 'black boxes'.
6. Q: What is the first step before opening a computer case?
A: Back up important data and power down the system.
7. Q: Why should you press and hold the power button after unplugging?
A: To drain any residual power from the power supply.
8. Q: What can be used to store screws during disassembly?
A: A plastic bag or cup.
9. Q: What might need to be removed before a side panel on a computer case?
A: The front faceplate.
10. Q: What should you clip your ESD strap to?
A: The metal side of the computer case.
11. Q: What components become visible after opening the computer case?
A: Power supply, motherboard, and drives.
12. Q: What should be done before removing any components?
A: Diagram all cable connections and label them if necessary.
13. Q: How should expansion cards be removed?
A: Remove attached cables, unscrew them, then lift the card straight up carefully.
14. Q: Where should expansion cards be stored once removed?
A: In antistatic bags.
15. Q: Why are spacers (standoffs) important for the motherboard?
A: They prevent the motherboard from touching the case and causing a short.
16. Q: In what order should components be reinstalled?
A: Power supply, drives, motherboard, and expansion cards.
17. Q: Why might it be easier to connect cables before inserting drives?
A: Cables can be hard to reach after the drives are fully installed.
18. Q: What is the main power connector for the motherboard?
A: The 24-pin P1 connector.
19. Q: What is the purpose of the auxiliary 4-pin power connector?
A: To supply power to the processor.
20. Q: What component should be connected to the "Fan Header" on the motherboard?
A: The case fan or CPU cooler.
21. Q: Why do laptop repairs usually cost more than desktops?
A: Laptop parts are smaller, often proprietary, and harder to replace.
22. Q: What is the first thing to check before servicing a laptop?
A: Whether the laptop is still under warranty.
23. Q: What information is needed when contacting technical support for a laptop?
A: Laptop model, serial number, purchaser details, and warranty status.
24. Q: Why are service manuals important when working on laptops?
A: They provide safe disassembly instructions and component locations.
25. Q: Where can a technician find a laptop's service manual?
A: Manufacturer's website, physical manuals, or third-party sites.
26. Q: What is one example of diagnostic software provided by manufacturers?
A: PC-Doctor (used by Lenovo, Fujitsu, HP).
27. Q: Why is working inside a laptop more complex than a desktop?
A: Laptops have smaller screws, tight spaces, and fragile components.
28. Q: What tools are required for disassembling a laptop?
A: Small screwdrivers, plastic pry tools, tweezers, and ESD protection.
29. Q: How should screws be organized during laptop disassembly?
A: Use a labeled pillbox, sticky notes, or tape screws next to documentation.
30. Q: What should be avoided to prevent voiding a laptop's warranty?
A: Forcing parts open, breaking warranty seals, or improper disassembly.
31. Q: What is a ZIF connector, and why is it important?
A: A Zero Insertion Force connector holds cables in place and must be unlocked carefully.
32. Q: What should be done before installing the battery after repair?
A: Ensure there are no loose parts or screws inside the laptop.
33. Q: What are common upgrades that might require opening a laptop?
A: Replacing RAM, hard drives, or a broken screen.
34. Q: What is the general tip for reassembling a laptop?
A: Reverse the order of disassembly and do not overtighten screws.
35. Q: Why might a laptop use one-time plastic screws?
A: They're designed to prevent reuse for security or warranty reasons.
36. Q: Why is it important to press the power button after unplugging a computer?
A: To drain residual power from the motherboard and components.
37. Q: What is the most common reason a reassembled PC won't turn on?
A: A loose connection.
38. Q: What alternative should be considered before repairing a damaged laptop?
A: Replacing the laptop or using external peripherals if possible.
39. Q: What is one benefit of ordering parts directly from the laptop manufacturer?
A: Compatibility assurance and warranty protection.
40. Q: What components typically connect to the front panel header of a motherboard?
A: Power switch, reset switch, HDD LED, power LED, and speaker.
41. Q: Why might you need to remove the power supply before the motherboard?
A: In some cases, the power supply blocks access to motherboard screws or components.
42. Q: How can you identify how the power supply is mounted?
A: Look for screws on the back and underside of the case and possible recessed slots.
43. Q: What mistake should you avoid when removing the power supply?
A: Do not remove screws that hold the power supply housing together.
44. Q: What is the proper way to remove internal drives like hard drives or optical drives?
A: Unscrew from both sides or release clips depending on the case and bay type.
45. Q: What might you need to remove before taking out a drive?
A: In some cases, the entire drive bay must be removed first.
46. Q: What should be double-checked before turning on the PC after reassembly?
A: All connections, especially power and front panel header cables.
47. Q: What connectors from the power supply must be plugged into the motherboard?
A: The 24-pin main connector and the 4-pin (or 8-pin) auxiliary CPU power connector.
48. Q: What is the purpose of the PCIe 6-pin or 8-pin connector?
A: To provide power to high-performance graphics cards.
49. Q: What's the best way to ensure correct front panel header connections?
A: Align pin 1 on the cable with pin 1 on the motherboard; consult the manual if unclear.
50. Q: How can you tell if the system is working after reassembly?
A: It powers on, the display works, and the system boots with no error beeps.
51. Q: Why must you wear an ESD strap when working inside a computer?
A: To prevent electrostatic discharge which can damage components.
52. Q: Why should you avoid working on carpeted floors when repairing PCs?
A: Carpet increases the risk of static buildup.
53. Q: What are 'black boxes' in computer repair?
A: Components like power supplies and monitors that should not be opened due to electrical risk.
54. Q: What is the safest way to handle circuit boards?
A: By their edges, avoiding contact with chips and connectors.
55. Q: What should you do if a screw drops inside a power supply unit?
A: Do not attempt to retrieve it-replace or professionally service the unit.
56. Q: What is a common symptom of a loose CPU power cable?
A: The system powers on but doesn't boot or shuts down immediately.
57. Q: What does a series of beeps at boot usually indicate?
A: A hardware error-refer to the motherboard manual for beep code meanings.
58. Q: What should be your first step if the PC doesn't power on after reassembly?
A: Check the power cable, power supply switch, and all internal connections.
59. Q: Why is labeling cables during disassembly helpful?
A: It speeds up and simplifies the reassembly process.
60. Q: What should you do if a component won't easily come out?
A: Double-check for screws or clips-never force it.
61. Q: When is replacing an entire laptop more practical than repairing it?
A: When repairs are complex and exceed the value of the laptop.
62. Q: How can external peripherals be used instead of internal replacements?
A: For example, using a USB keyboard instead of replacing a broken internal one.
63. Q: What's a good strategy to avoid misplacing laptop screws during disassembly?
A: Tape them to a labeled diagram or use a magnetic mat with sections.
64. Q: What connector type is commonly used for laptop keyboards?
A: ZIF (Zero Insertion Force) connectors.
65. Q: Why is patience crucial during laptop disassembly?
A: Components are fragile, compact, and can break easily with force.
66. Q: What general rule applies to both desktops and laptops before repair?
A: Always disconnect power and discharge static electricity.
67. Q: What should be used to track screw locations for both desktops and laptops?
A: Notes, diagrams, or labeled containers.
68. Q: Why should thermal paste be applied when replacing a CPU?
A: To ensure proper heat transfer between CPU and heatsink.
69. Q: What should be verified before powering on after any internal work?
A: No tools or screws are left inside the case, and all parts are securely in place.
70. Q: What is the role of the front panel I/O connectors?
A: They connect the case's buttons, LEDs, and ports to the motherboard.
71. Q: Why should you use original brand parts for laptops?
A: To ensure compatibility and maintain warranty coverage.
72. Q: Where can diagnostic tools for laptops usually be found?
A: Manufacturer websites, included CDs, or recovery partitions.
73. Q: What kind of information can be found in a laptop service manual?
A: Disassembly steps, part locations, and troubleshooting guidance.
74. Q: What should be done before ordering laptop parts?
A: Identify the exact model and verify compatibility.
75. Q: What type of support is best for complex repairs under warranty?
A: Sending the laptop to an authorized service center.
76. Q: What's the biggest difference between desktop and laptop servicing?
A: Laptops are more compact, fragile, and harder to disassemble.
77. Q: What components are usually easiest to upgrade in laptops?
A: RAM and storage (SSD/HDD).
78. Q: Why is static discharge a bigger risk with modern components?
A: They're more sensitive and operate at lower voltages.
79. Q: What is the best way to prepare for a hands-on certification exam?
A: Practice real hardware disassembly and follow ESD precautions.
80. Q: What is the golden rule of computer hardware servicing?
A: Document everything and never force components into place.
